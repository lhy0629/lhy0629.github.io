About me

I am a second-year PhD student at School of Mathematical Sciences, Peking University, advised by Xin Sun. Previously, I earned my B.Sc. in Mathematics and B.Ec. in Economics from Peking University, where I did undergraduate research with Jian Ding.

My research interests lie in probability theory, especially random geometry and statistical physics. In partitular, I focus on percolation theory, Potts models, random cluster models, Schramm-Loewner evolution and their interplay with Liouville quantum gravity.

I was born and raised in Hangzhou, and in my free time, I enjoy swimming and playing squash.

My Erdős number is 4.
